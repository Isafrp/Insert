On Supporting the Internet of Things Through Named Data Networking: Interoperability, Security, and Mobility.

Leobino Sampaio, Francisco Renato Araújo, Ítalo Valcy Brito, Mateus Sousa, Guilherme Araújo

Last updated on September 1, 2020

Despite the benefits and potential of NDN architectures in addressing communication challenges between network devices in IoT, many research and development issues remain open and are still in the early stages of discussion. Thus, the main objective of this research is to define and implement architectural elements of NDN that provide native support for device communication in the Internet of Things, addressing interoperability, mobility, and security. This general objective unfolds into the following specific goals: i. The development of confidentiality and integrity mechanisms for network content; ii. Support for the mobility of data-producing and data-consuming nodes; iii. The definition of an adaptation layer that supports multiple transmission technologies at the link layer.
