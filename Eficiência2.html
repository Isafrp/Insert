Balancing Energy, Redundancy, and Performance in Data Center Networks

Leobino Nascimento Sampaio, Artur Ziviani, Antônio Cleber de Sousa Araújo, Dimitri Paixão dos Anjos, Ákila Luz Fernandes

Status: Ongoing

Modern large-scale data centers typically adopt server and communication equipment redundancy to enhance their reliability and availability. One of the main challenges in this field is the high energy consumption of these highly redundant infrastructures. This project investigates the development of an energy-efficient strategy for data center networks based on the Fat-Tree topology. The strategy leverages multipath routing and the global view provided by software-defined networking (SDN) to balance energy efficiency, equipment redundancy levels, and performance gains in handling traffic demands.
